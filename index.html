What is SDG 8?

Sustainable Development Goal 8 aims to promote sustained, inclusive, and sustainable economic growth, full and productive employment, and decent work for all by 2030. For the Philippines, this means addressing unemployment, underemployment, informal labor, and ensuring fair wages and safe working conditions.

The Philippines has made strides in economic growth (averaging 6.4% GDP growth from 2010-2019), but challenges remain in translating growth into quality jobs for all Filipinos.

Challenges

  • High underemployment (Workers seeking longer hours or better jobs)
  • Large informal sector (Lack of social protection and benefits)
  • Skills mismatch (Education attained by graduatees are not aligned with industry needs)
  • Unstable employment (Seasonal work, Low wage jobs)
  • Impact of global crisis (Pandemics, High Inflation, and Natural Disasters)

Government & Private Sector Initiatives

  • TUPAD Program: Temporary employment for disaster affected workers.
  • SSS/PhilHealth/Pag-IBIG: Expanding social protection to informal sector workers.
  • Technical Education and Skills Development Authority (TESDA): Skills training for in-demand jobs (IT, Healthcare, Construction, and Cookery).
  • DOLE JobStart Philippines: Youth employment program with training and internships.
  • Private Sector Partnerships: Companies adopting fair labor practices and trainee programs.
